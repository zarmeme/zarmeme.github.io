About me

A bit curious,
a bit technical.

"I make things that feel alive. That's the whole job."

I'm a creative technologist with 5 years building experiences at the edge of design and code. My sweet spot is the gap — where rigorous UX systems and generative, expressive ideas overlap into something genuinely surprising.

Before tech I studied fine art, which means I came to code from the other direction — thinking first about composition, rhythm, and what things feel like, then figuring out how to build them. It's a useful order.

I prototype fast, iterate with a lot of joy, and believe the best work comes from treating every micro-interaction as its own design problem.

  • 5+ yrs across agencies, startups, and museum installations
  • Fine art background — came to programming from visual thinking
  • Comfortable as the only designer on an engineering team (and vice versa)
  • Open to full-time, freelance, and the occasional weird experiment
  • I build prototypes to think, not just to validate — code is my sketchbook
  • Delight is a mechanism, not decoration
  • Systems first, aesthetics second — but never last
  • Best ideas start with "what if we just…"
  • Currently obsessed with reaction-diffusion systems and computational poetry
  • I read ~40 books a year: sci-fi, design theory, and anything weird
  • Made my first website at 11 — Comic Sans, animated GIFs, hit counter. Peak design.
  • I have strong opinions about kerning. I'm sorry. I'm not sorry.
